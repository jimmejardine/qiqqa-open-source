Highlighter Misbehavior

Why is the highlighter so finicky? It consistently highlights text, sometimes entire paragraphs, that are not directly under the cursor. This makes the feature difficult and frustrating to use.
6580786
  • 0
    Dennis over 5 years ago
    One possibility is that the highlighter highlights the OCR'd text, even though you can see the scanned text. If the OCR didn't do a good job finding text, then you cannot highlight it.

    If there is already OCR text before the import to Qiqqa, then Qiqqa uses that. My scanner came with ABBYY Finereader which seems to do a much better job than the OCR that comes with Qiqqa, so I have it do the OCR and include it in the PDF before the import to Qiqqa.
  • 0
    Michael Tolston over 5 years ago
    Thanks for the reply, Dennis. One thing that makes me think OCR isn't the problem is the fact that I can easily select text using the cursor, but highlighting still poses problems. I've also used external (Adobe Acrobat) OCR on several of my PDFs when Qiqqa's OCR fails to do a proper job.
    Have you never experienced any funky highlighting behavior using Qiqqa's own PDF viewer and annotation tools?
  • 1
    Dennis over 5 years ago
    Yeah, I guess it can be described as "funky" in the sense that it doesn't behave like a physical highlighter would.

    Left clicking on a word highlights that word. If the left button is held down, as the pointer moves, every word the pointer touches is highlighted as well as every word in the text stream between the original word and the any word touched before the mouse button is released.

    The times it gets "funky" for me tend to be when the OCR messes up the text order. A typical case seems to be when the OCR treats a two column page as one column. (This also totally screws up citation cross references).
  • 0
    Michael Tolston over 5 years ago
    I understand how digital highlighters work. This is just the worst one, by far, that I have ever seen.
  • 1
    Dennis over 5 years ago
    @Michael

    My expectation was that a highlighter will highlight word by word as the the mouse pointer touches words with the left mouse button down.

    Qiqqa's highlighter will highlight all words in the text stream between the first position and the last position in the text stream touched by the mouse pointer with the left mouse button down.

    Very different behavior which seems especially funky if the OCR mangles the order of text stream.

    Dennis over 5 years ago
    For those who just can't stand the Qiqqa highlighter, alternative procedure to do highlighting in Adobe Acrobat:

    In library view:
    1. Right click on PDF you want to highlight.
    2. Open > Open in external PDF viewer (assumes Adobe Acrobat default viewer)
    3. Highlight article using Acrobat tools.
    4. Save highlighted PDF and close.
    5. In Qiqqa, right click on PDF you just highlighted.
    6. Misc > Import legacy PDF annotations

    Not official, but seems to work ok.
