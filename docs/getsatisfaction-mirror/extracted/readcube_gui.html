Readcube GUI

While I think the features in this program are by far the best, I think the look and feel of the program are a little bulky. One merely has to look to all of the competitors to check out their GUI to instantly determine that Qiqqa is busy-looking. I highly recommend looking at ReadCube for simplicity in interface. Thus, while Qiqqa has features that others are just now starting to think of, ReadCube (and others) have more intuitive functionality and interface. I recommend a revamping of the entire interface while maintaining the features.

    TCS over 8 years ago
    Out of interest, I took a look at ReadCube. It does look pretty, but ( without exaggeration) I would estimate that even after a year since this original posting, ReadQube does not even have 25% of the functionality that Qiqqa has.

    Though I would agree that Qiqqa could improve their interface, my worry is that there is also a cost for making it too simple... that access to functions becomes more hierarchical, and actions begin to take more mouse clicks to complete, or that complex functionality is sacrificed for UI. Also, there is a cost - of what doesn't get done while there is focus on UI. At this stage of Qiqqa development, I'd much rather see Qiqqa's finite resources directed towards continuing to pioneer the functionality that they have; such as:

    * Tagging categorization
    * Better contextual search and mining of the data (!tags, +tags, frequency reporting etc)
    * Expand and contracting within brain storming
    ... and other great suggestions that I've seen recently

    My hope is that Qiqqa doesn't "loose steam", that they continue to expand on the functionality that they have... they kindof opened a pandora's box when they responded to users requests for functionality, as they have. Once users see someone get a request implemented, then they want their requests implemented... and things can start to sound quite negative.

      The header for this thread was ReadCude, therefore I compared it to ReadCube.

      But, if we switch the discussion to Mendeley ... I believe the argument still holds...
      1) ReadCude is "prettier" than Mendely.
      2) ReadCude has fewer features than Mendeley.
      3) Mendely is "prettier" than Qiqqa
      4) Mendely has fewer features/functions then Qiqqa.
      therefore, Qiqqa is relatively "ugly", but has more features :)

      Don't get me wrong, I hope they can improve the "prettiness" of the Qiqqa interface AND not slow down the development of innovative features that continue to set it apart from other software... but if there is a trade-off... at this stage of Qiqqa development/maturity, I'd hope that Qiqqa developers choose the later option.

      I think it's admirable though - that you feel the Mendeley has equivalent features and a better interface, but you choose Qiqqa. I think good user involvement is critical to continued Qiqqa development and progress.
