Undoing v52 citation duplicate problem in Word 2013

In a rush this morning I updated by dissertation references using v.52 and Word 2013, saved the document, and ran out the door. This afternoon I discovered that all my citations have multiple entries. I have since upgraded to v.54, but how do I get rid of all of those duplicates? If I tell v.54 to reformat the document, I just get more duplicate citations.
Answered
5200341
  • EMPLOYEE
    1
    Qiqqa (Official Rep) over 7 years ago
    Microsoft changed the way they use fields in Word 2013, which causes pre-v53 Qiqqa to insert citation text next to the field instead of inside the field. If you wave the wand a second time, it does this insertion again, causing the duplicate.

    Unfortunately this Qiqqa-inserted text is not distinguishable from your normal written text (it is not in the field!) so there is no automatic way for Qiqqa to fix it.

    From v53 onwards Qiqqa now detects Word 2013 and does things differently with it.

    All I can suggest is checking if you had version tracking on, and reverting to the Word document version before you refreshed your citations. Or to go to your backups for an older version.

    Alternatively, you will have to manually remove the "extra text". To quickly jump from citation to citation in word, fire up Qiqqa InCite and press the "Show the used citations" button (the binoculars). Then you can click on each citation in turn to be taken there in Word and quickly fix the text.

    Hope it helps!
