I am concerned about privacy. Where do I stand with Qiqqa?

    Qiqqa (Official Rep) over 9 years ago
    If you do not use the syncing feature of Qiqqa, you can rest assured that your documents will remain local and private to you and your computer at all times. We will add in future the ability to completely disable Syncing, so that you don't sync by accident.

    If you are comfortable that PDFs on your local computer offer you enough privacy, then you can use Qiqqa locally without additional concern.

    That said, even if you have synced to your Web Library, we do not make your documents available to anyone else, unless you explicitly invite them to your Library.

    We use Amazon's Cloud Service to store your Web Libraries. These are private and not accessible to anyone else unless you share the library explicitly. You can find out more about Amazon's top-notch security and privacy standards here.

    If you have any ideas about how we might be able to further improve Qiqqa in a way that offers you further peace of mind, don't hesitate to give us that feedback. We would love to solve your problem!

    NotPinky (Company Admin) over 8 years ago
    Hi Alberto,

    You can perhaps understand more of the reasons for this from my answer here:

    https://getsatisfaction.com/qiqqa/top...

    As per my answer in that thread, these terms and conditions are there to allows us, perhaps, in future, to provide totally anonymous, *aggregated* services (e.g. recommendations about what to read), or personalised services to you specifically (e.g. doing computations on your documents on our servers, so that your computer doesn't have to). If you review the wording, you'll see the license is purely so that we can use the content to provide the services mentioned. In neither case would your data be explicitly available to anyone else.

    I'm not a lawyer (so don't hold me to this!), but from what I understand, the "worldwide license" aspect is legal wording so that we are able to host our servers in a different jurisdiction to you, and still provide those services mentioned (we are based in the U.K, our customers are all over the world).

    We have no interest in exposing your data in any way that would be surprising to you, or making our users unhappy. (I can't even imagine a business model that would work along those lines!) Our intent is to provide a really great, useful service to our users. Meeting their expecations in terms of keeping their data secure and private is part of that.

    Other options: We do have customers that for legal or some other reasons are not allowed to sync with an external service like ours - we are working on an option to allow them to sync locally. And of course, if you still feel that the above does not work for you for any reason, you can still very effectively use Qiqqa without syncing, and your documents will never leave your computer. Syncing is provided as a convenience for users with multiple devices.

    Hope that helps, thanks for your query Alberto.
