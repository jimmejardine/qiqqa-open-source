Qiqqa is so fast, Google Scholar thinks I'm a bot!

I'm just getting started with Qiqqa and like what I see so far!

I imported a large number of pdf files for a paper that I'm writing and was rapidly going through them associating the correct BibTex with each pdf. After getting through about half of them, Google has blocked access to my ip address thinking that I'm used some sort of automated method to access the Scholar website. Is this something that's been reported before? Any idea how many articles one can look up on Google Scholar with the sniffer before it locks up?

    I ran into this problem myself after moving in about 200 PDFs. I'm not sure the exact number google stopped me at though.

    Since then if I'm doing more than 10 PDFs I don't use the "BibTex Sniffer Wizard" (magic hat under the arrow). I think the cause is that the wizard searches the title and then instantly grabs the first record into the BibTex. I think its the speed between those two actions repeated more than a few times sends up bot alarms at the googleplex.

    Qiqqa (Official Rep) over 9 years ago
    Yeah - unfortunately, but understandably, Google Scholar does not like bots scraping their website. It is one of the reason we went for the semi-automatic BibTeX Sniffer (the other is because we feel you want to make sure the BibTeX is 100% correct before relying on it).

    So if you are working through your PDFs so quickly that Google Scholar does block you, give it a rest for an hour or so and try again. It's great that the guys at Google are offering this cool resource, so who's complaining anyway :-)
